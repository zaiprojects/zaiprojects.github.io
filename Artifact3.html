The Lights

like fairy lights.

like fireworks on a summer evening.

or maybe lamp posts on a suburban street.

ooh or maybe christmas lights!

it's not really important which light this artifact is. it's all electromagnetic energy. its all heat. have you felt that heat? its nice. the warmth surrounds you like the universe is bathing you in its power. but it's not really scary. feels like comfort. feels like understanding. when you just sit outside and listen. ironically, when you stop looking around, the light becomes a more comforting force. in those moments, it's not just a utility, its a peace that fills the air. light is how most people take in information. we make machines to beam specific light right into our brains. our eyes are so close to the brain it's hard to differentiate them biologically. we learn through light. we are entertained by light. light makes people live, and light makes people die.

that is the moment i feel the most understood. that is why the light is one of these artifacts. it's the thing that understands me better than any other human. religion is a tricky subject for me. i often rely on the spiritual experiences brought to me by light and nature. i grew up in the church. hymns and bible verses were comfort to me. in some ways they still are. my belief often falters. but my belief returns in those times. those times trigger both a godly and human comfort those times sitting outside among the man made light sources. i have a constant powerlessness in my chest, but the light reminds me of what humanity does. the light is hope, at least for some. we are lost flesh of atoms on atoms among nothing. but that dark nothing is defeatable in some ways. we have bent the universe to our will. we have created beasts that glow as bright as stars when close to our face. they do not burn. they just keep glowing. the night is our evolutionary enemy. the winter is our evolutionary enemy. we have defeated them with our intellect. we desperately put together materials and substances until something works. through that determination we have conquered the night. not in its entirety, mind you. like me, humans cannot commit to any single ideal forever. but, for now, we have the power and grit to banish the cold. and we choose to. for now.











AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

fight back. i need to fight back. please don't let the darkness fill my arteries like a spiderweb. it will clog my bodily functions. it causes my muscles to seize and tremor. i hate it. im afraid of being dragged back down into the mud. i've been there before many times. the tale is going to repeat. i knew that i would be back here. why did i ever think that i escaped. i cannot climb out with a ladder, vessel, or wings. nothing is working. the dark settles in as the gravity of the mind causes it to full the space i leave open. i need the light to stay around me. please i need the light. i need to fight back. fight back.

we scream. like a child lost in the dark. we see the nothingness and we reject it. that scream is the same as what the light provides me. i rebel against the creeping meaninglessness. i scream. i cry. i try not to forget what it means to care about things and people. its a raw, almost painful, sensual grounding. psychology teaches us humans about grounding. it's such a tragedy that one of the best ways to calm our mind is to silence it. we cannot make noise and be comforted. we need to accept the nothingness without accepting the darkness. that's what the light is for. whether my heart is pumping from physical exercise or calm from restful sounds, i am reassured. in the light i know that i am a body. i am somewhere. the universe is assuring me of my existence. and often, that is enough. just knowing i am is enough. the light is a reminder for me.
