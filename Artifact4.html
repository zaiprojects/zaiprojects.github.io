The Water

like a river.

like an enormous waterfall in the middle of nowhere.

or a faucet someone is leaning over while hating their reflection.

ooh, or maybe it's contained in plastic and shipped far, far away.

it's not really important what water this artifact is. water is fluid. like my identity. shout out to the fellow LGBTQIA+ people. water changes based on its surrounding. it fills whatever shape it's in. despite this, it still possess power and movement.

the water does not care. it does not want for love. it does not represent an existential dissonance pounding away at itself. it flows down. i envy the waters flow. in my existence i try to replicate the lack of shape. it can do feats greater than any living thing, but i repeat myself. i know i cannot be in the water for long. like all else, i would eventually be torn apart. from water, to water. drowning is one of the most common fears. just like the lights, you can be bathed in water. however, unlike the lights, the immersion of the body into water doesn't relax us. it brings that fear of death right to the front of our consciousness. those fearful moments are when meaning really starts to matter to us. we think about it abstractly, but as you sink below the surface, you realize just how real meaning can be. we are searching for meaning to appease that monster within us. it's one we can only really see when we are in grave danger, but it hangs over us all the time.

all of this is about happiness, right? why else would we looking for meaning? i'm not really interested in finding out some kind of “Truth” if that idea poisons my mind with hatred and despair. why else would we be looking for meaning? wouldn't it be better to just be like the water? just flow. no reactions. no more hatred. no more suffering. no more anything. that's where we all end up anyway right? why keep it at bay. why build dams if they are to collapse and let the torrent through? why? Why? WHY?

yeah. that's why the water is here. it's a scary thought, for sure. sorry if that was upsetting, but in my defence, it also upset me. the ocean of death certainly does wait for us, and the dam will someday break. truthfully, i wish i could provide you with some solace after that. truthfully, i wish i could provide myself some solace in this moment. i don't have a reason for you. maybe it's just the fear talking, but i'm definitely happy that i haven't listened to the allure of the riverbed in a while. keep rowing friend.

well, as a little refresher for you, here's a glass. the water also represents my social abilities. there is strength in being able to roll with the proverbial punches of everyday conversation. as someone who grew up extremely isolated from their piers, i never really developed the proper social barriers or habits. when i was thrust into the throw of socialization that is university life, i was worried that i wouldn't be able to keep up with my piers. and yet, just by allowing myself to continue flowing around each conversation, i have been able to thrive. i thank the river for that.
