Conclusion

Now is the time for me to describe meaning where there is none. I will draw conclusions upon faint emotions and images as if I was weaving with dissolving spider silk. The worldview that takes form from this discontinuous collection of artifacts is one of hollowness. Meaning that derives from nothing. It describes learning how to persist on temporary ideologies day by day, and the screeching that it takes to constantly switch fundamental beliefs about yourself and the universe around you. This worldview is a patchwork quilt I am knitting with one hand as the other hand unwinds the threads at the other side. There is a high probability that I will look back at this website in the future and desire to tear it down and start again. However, I hope that you can feel some of what it means to be in this moment.

It is sometimes required to scream. Despite this supposed emptiness, I don’t let it dull my emotions. In fact, without concrete beliefs to muffle the noise, I can listen to my body more closely. It’s very easy for me to find pleasure in things like the smell of fresh food or the sound of music when I don’t put any preconceived notions onto my relationships with these things. Similarly, when I do find myself trapped in a myth or belief, I no longer look to destroy it with socratic irony. I know that day will happen soon anyway. Instead, I can relish in the euphoria of committing to something or someone for a short period. I do not need to second guess these feelings and the actions they bring out of me because I know that I will lose faith in them in time. In some ways, I could see this being perceived as self destructive or ignorant. Of course, I don’t usually want to hurt the people around me with my words, but I believe it is better to be truthful to your immediate mind and body. No idea can be consistent with itself, so engage with other people as if that does not matter.

From inside the void of my mind I can reach out in any direction. That is to say, my worldview is defined both in not being attracted to any ideas, but also not being repelled from any ideas. When I meet other people, when I read stories, when I listen to the bugs and the wind, I feel like I can understand them, even if just for a second. I can sit listening to the sounds of the environment and lose myself so much that I need to actively remind myself that I am an active participant in the universe, not just a mere onlooker. It may be the case that this experience is what people mean when they describe ego death, but I find that word violent. For me, it means having the capacity to let go.

I say all of these things not to imply that there are no consistent throughlines in my life, but those rare consistencies cannot be expressed through regular language. Instead, I try to communicate about them through experience. If I want to share who I am with a friend or reader, I must do so through making them feel how I feel, not simply explaining myself. Even now, as I try to lay out my feelings, I find myself believing these words to be totally incomplete. This carries through to every aspect of my life, from friendships and relationships to media and art. In some regard, I believe all humans live inside their head like this. We are looking at the external world through the viewscreen of our senses. There is no way to ever truly let someone into your mind. We cannot be understood.

Thank you, reader for your attention, for your eyesight. As much as I resent putting my feelings into language, it is likely helpful for me to be forced to do so. If you read this and felt that you could relate to much of it, we see each other at this moment. If you read this and felt that you were confused or frustrated, I see you as well.
